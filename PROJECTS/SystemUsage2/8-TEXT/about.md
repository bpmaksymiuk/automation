# About - The Boiler Room

Most system monitors hand you a wall of numbers. The Boiler Room hands you a machine.

Stand at the operator's station and read your computer the way an engineer reads an engine room - by its temperament. A glance tells you whether the machine is resting, working, or straining. The furnace brightens as the processor labours. The boiler's water climbs from a simmer to a rolling boil as memory fills. Pressure tanks hold your disks; glowing steam runs the network pipe. When a gauge passes the limit you set, a relief valve vents with a hiss - the machine protecting itself, not scolding you.

Every flame, bubble, and particle is bound to a true measurement. The beauty is the data. Numbers are always one hover away, and the room is pleasant enough to leave running on a second screen - calm by default, loud only when it matters.
